Delicacy Fruits in the Philippines

At CEBUTA Homemade Jam, we are proud to bring you the authentic and time-honored flavors of the Philippines, carefully crafted in the heart of our kitchen. Our homemade Filipino delicacies are a tribute to the rich culinary heritage of the Philippines, each bite taking you on a journey through the vibrant and diverse tapestry of Filipino cuisine.

Our homemade Filipino delicacies are lovingly prepared, following traditional recipes passed down through generations. We use the finest and freshest ingredients, combined with time-tested techniques, ensuring that every morsel is a true representation of Filipino culinary artistry.



Mangga

Mangga, known as mango in English, holds a special place in the hearts and palates of Filipinos. This tropical fruit, revered for its succulent flesh and sweet aroma, is an iconic symbol of the Philippines' rich agricultural heritage. Let's delve into the fascinating world of mangga fruit in the Philippines.

Mangga fruits are typically oval or kidney-shaped, with a smooth, golden skin that may exhibit shades of green and red when ripe. Once ripened, the fruit gives off an enticing, tropical fragrance, signaling its readiness for consumption. Inside, the mango conceals a juicy, orange-yellow flesh that is both sweet and mildly tangy. It's a true delight for the senses.

Scientific name: Mangifera indica L. Filipino name: Mangga Mango season in the Philippines: March to June



Atis

The atis fruit, also known as "sugar apple" or "custard apple," is a tropical fruit that is commonly found in the Philippines. It belongs to the Annona family of fruits and is known for its sweet and creamy flesh. Here are some key characteristics and information about the atis fruit in the Philippines.

The atis fruit typically has a green, scaly, and knobby skin, which is usually not edible. The skin is often broken or peeled away to reveal the sweet, white, and custard-like flesh inside. The flesh of the atis is divided into segments, each containing a shiny black seed. The seeds are not consumed, and the flesh is scooped or bitten directly off the seeds.

Scientific name: Annona squamosa Filipino name: Atis Custard apple season in the Philippines: September to December


Aratilis

Aratilis, scientifically known as Muntingia calabura, is a charming and exotic fruit that is indigenous to the Philippines. This small, cherry-like berry is a beloved local delicacy, and its unique combination of flavors and cultural significance makes it a fascinating part of Filipino culinary heritage.

Aratilis berries are small, typically about the size of a cherry, with thin, delicate skin. They are usually bright red or orange when ripe, but can also be yellow or purple, depending on the variety and ripeness. The fruit is best known for its sweet and mildly tangy flavor. Aratilis is a fruit associated with childhood memories for many Filipinos. It is often depicted in local literature and songs, evoking a sense of nostalgia.

Scientific name: Muntingia calabura Filipino name: Aratilis Muntingia season in the Philippines: March to June




Guyabano

Guyabano, known scientifically as Annona muricata and commonly referred to as soursop or graviola, is an exotic tropical fruit that thrives in the Philippines. Renowned for its unique flavor and potential health benefits, the guyabano is a cherished fruit that has been part of Philippine culinary traditions for generations.

Guyabano's flavor is an intriguing blend of sweet and tart, often described as a combination of pineapple, strawberry, and citrus notes. The guyabano is a large, spiky, green fruit with soft, fibrous flesh inside. The skin is typically prickly and not edible. The flesh is pale green, almost white, and is characterized by its custard-like texture.

Scientific name: Annona muricata Filipino name: Guyabano Soursop season in the Philippines: August to November


Balimbing

Balimbing, scientifically known as Averrhoa carambola, is a tropical fruit commonly referred to as "star fruit" due to its distinctive five-pointed shape when sliced crosswise. Indigenous to the Philippines and other parts of Southeast Asia, balimbing is celebrated for its unique appearance, crisp texture, and sweet-tangy flavor.

Balimbing is easily recognizable by its star-shaped cross-section, which makes it an appealing addition to fruit platters and desserts. The fruit's skin is smooth, waxy, and often yellow or light green, with a slight sheen. It is edible and has a mild flavor. The flesh is pale yellow and typically sweet with a hint of tartness, although flavor can vary among different cultivars.

Scientific name: Averrhoa carambola Filipino name: Balimbing Star fruit season in the Philippines: May to August


Pinya

Pinya, known as pineapple in English, is a vibrant tropical fruit cherished in the Philippines and around the world. Renowned for its sweet and tangy flavor, pineapple is a symbol of hospitality and a versatile ingredient in Filipino cuisine.

Pinya has a spiky, rough exterior with a pattern of hexagonal-shaped "eyes." Inside, the fruit features juicy, golden-yellow flesh and a tough core. Pinya can be enjoyed fresh by slicing it into rings, chunks, or spears. It is a popular addition to fruit salads, juices, and smoothies, enhancing their flavor and natural sweetness. Pinya is often used in cooking, featuring in savory dishes like the Filipino favorite "hamonado" (sweet ham) and adding a delightful tropical twist to pizza and burgers.

Scientific name: Ananas comosus Filipino name: Pinya Pineapple season in the Philippines: May to July




Chico

Chico fruit has an oval or round shape with a rough, grainy, and brownish skin. The flesh of the chico is typically a warm, peachy color, and it's incredibly sweet and aromatic. Inside, the fruit contains dark, inedible seeds, which are removed before consumption.

Chico is best enjoyed fresh, with the flesh scooped out and savored directly. Its exquisite taste and grainy texture make it a wonderful addition to fruit salads and desserts. Chico can also be used to create jams, jellies, and beverages. Chico is a source of dietary fiber, vitamins (such as vitamin C), and essential minerals. The fruit is believed to have potential health benefits, such as aiding digestion and supporting the immune system.

Scientific name: Manilkara zapota Filipino name: Chico Sapodilla season in the Philippines: January and February



Santol

Santol, scientifically known as Sandoricum koetjape but often called "wild mangosteen," is a captivating tropical fruit celebrated in the Philippines for its tangy-sweet flavor and versatility in various culinary applications. Native to the country, santol is a true tropical gem.

Santol is a round or oval fruit with a thin, leathery skin that ranges in color from green to bright yellow. The flesh of the santol can vary from white to pale yellow or orange, and it is typically segmented, like an orange or grapefruit. The taste of santol is a delightful blend of sweet and sour. Santol is commonly enjoyed fresh by biting into the flesh or peeling it away from the seeds. The fruit's rind can be used to make a delicious and tangy preserve or jam.

Scientific name: Sandoricum koetjape Filipino name: Santol Santol season in the Philippines: July to September


Bayabas

Bayabas, scientifically known as Psidium guajava but often referred to as "guava," is a delightful tropical fruit cherished in the Philippines for its sweet and tangy flavor, versatility, and numerous health benefits. This fruit, native to the country, is a true tropical wonder.

Bayabas fruit is typically round or pear-shaped with green skin that may have a slight blush when ripe. The flesh inside is commonly white or pink and often contains small, edible seeds. The taste of bayabas is a combination of sweetness and tartness, often described as a blend of pear, strawberry, and citrus notes. Bayabas is frequently enjoyed fresh by slicing it into sections and biting directly into the flesh. It is a popular choice for making refreshing beverages.

Scientific name: Psidium guajava Filipino name: Bayabas Guava season in the Philippines: September to December
